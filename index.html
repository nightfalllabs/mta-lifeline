Prehospital Trauma Decision Trainer
Training aid — not a medical device. LifeLine is an educational simulation for classroom, academy, and self-study use. Its mechanics are inspired by publicly taught doctrine — TCCC, PHTLS, the Canadian Red Cross Emergency Medical Responder curriculum, and Stop the Bleed. It simplifies real physiology and is not a medical device, not for clinical decision-making, and not a substitute for a certified course with hands-on instruction. It collects no patient information and is not a record of competency on its own. If it gets you into a real class — that's the win condition.
For instructors. The lobby's Scenario Options calibrate difficulty for a cohort: pediatric patients, blind (undifferentiated) presentation, and a no-scaffold mode that hides the handoff checklist. Each run produces a copyable session record for your own gradebook. Set an optional operator or cohort ID below so those records are attributable.

CAMPAIGN — THE COMPETENCY LADDER

Thirteen lessons from bystander basics to the lethal triad, sequenced the way the real curricula sequence them — IFRC first aid → Stop the Bleed → TCCC tiers. Each lesson briefs you, then grades you. Your first pass credits your mastery profile and can unlock the matching free-play tier.

FREE PLAY — DRILL & CHECK-RIDE

Drill any unlocked tier freely — unrated practice, with the AAR timeline rewind. Switch on Assessment to take a rated check-ride: it credits your mastery profile, and clearing the next locked tier (score ≥ 70) unlocks it. Skill that goes undrilled rusts, so the profile reflects what you can do now.

Mass-Casualty Incident — Triage

One of you, many of them, and a finite Golden Hour. Walk the scene, read it from a distance, and sort — START/SALT. Triage is not treatment: tourniquet and airway only, then tag and move. Under-triage costs lives.

SERVICE RECORD

Every run, every save, every patient lost. Readiness is a snapshot of what you can do right now — it decays when a skill goes undrilled, and it does not equate to real-world qualification or the feel of holding pressure on a real wound.

Readiness Board

What you can be trusted to do right now, domain by domain. The band is your demonstrated capability; currency is whether the reps are recent enough to still count. A skill drops a band and flags as lapsing when it rusts — capability and currency are not the same thing.

Case Log

Readiness Levels

The level up top is the mean of your six domains, expressed as the supervision your demonstrated skill would warrant — the language clinical educators use, not a competitive rank. The scope anchors mirror the real bystander-to-critical-care ladder so progress means something. This is a self-assessed practice signal: no simulation can grant a licence, a certification, or a scope of practice.
